# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Businesses in Egypt and the UAE that want to grow fast on social media — e-commerce brands, local businesses, startups, and established companies that need viral content, UGC, or media buying to scale. They are growth-focused, results-oriented, and often skeptical of agencies that overpromise. Many are Arabic-speaking or operate in bilingual environments. They browse on their phones; the mobile experience is the primary experience.

## Product Purpose

The Reel Recipe is a full-service content agency that helps MENA brands dominate social media through content creation, user-generated content, and performance media buying. The site exists to convert skeptical business owners into clients by projecting authority, demonstrating results, and making it effortless to reach out. Success looks like a brand decision-maker landing on the page and immediately knowing: these people get results, they know this region, and I want to work with them.

## Brand Personality

Viral. Trustworthy. Relentless.

The voice is confident without being loud, direct without being cold. It speaks to business owners who have been burned by agencies before — it earns trust through proof, not promises. In Arabic it should feel native, not translated.

## Anti-references

Should not look AI-generated: no identical card grids, no generic hero-metric templates, no gradient text, no side-stripe borders. Nothing that a viewer could immediately categorize as "another agency template." The design should surprise — choices should feel deliberate and human, not assembled from a component library.

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over promises.** Stats, results, and social proof do the persuading. Copy should be sparse and factual. Let numbers and work carry weight.
2. **Arabic is a first language, not a fallback.** RTL layout and Arabic typography must be treated with the same design care as the English side. No awkward reflows, no afterthought placement.
3. **Mobile is the site.** The audience lives on their phones. Every section must be designed phone-first, with desktop as the expanded version.
4. **Human signature.** Every design decision — layout, type choice, motion, color — should feel like a person made it on purpose. If it could have been generated, rework it.
5. **Agency authority.** Reference: monks.com, dentsu.com. The tone is senior and established, not scrappy-startup. Confidence comes from restraint, not excess.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Full bilingual support: English (LTR) and Arabic (RTL). Arabic must use appropriate typefaces and correct directional layout, not just `dir="rtl"` on a Latin-optimized layout.
- Mobile-optimized at every breakpoint — phone-first design.
- Reduced motion: respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations.
- Color contrast: WCAG AA minimum across both language modes.
